**Root cause of the false-green risk (confirmed, now closed):** `chatBubbleMotionSelfTest.ts`'s `analyzeChatBubbleMotionSamples` only ever ran its **monotonic-descent shape check** (`isContinuousEase` / `continuousY`) against the isolated Motion **transform** `y`. A transform tween and a scroll tween that each ease smoothly on their own but drift slightly out of phase frame-to-frame can produce a composited position that oscillates (e.g. -2,+3,-4,+2 px) entirely inside that 10px band — self-test green, human eye reads jitter. **Fix:** Added `layoutYSeries()` + `continuousLayoutY` to `chatBubbleMotionSelfTest.ts`, gated in each bubble's `ok` and surfaced as its own named check (`layout-continuous`) in the checks array — not buried inside `bubbles-ok`. Proven with a new unit test (`chatBubbleMotionSelfTest.test.ts` "FAIL on layoutY co-travel wobble…") that constructs exactly this in-band-wobble fixture and confirms the **old** gate would have passed it while the **new** gate correctly fails it. Verified non-regressive against the live production bundle above (`layout-continuous: 7/7 ok`).
